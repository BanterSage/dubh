Second French Empire History

How It Began (1852)

Early Rule: Authoritarian Empire (1852-1860)

At first, Napoleon III ruled in an authoritarian way:

Limited press freedom

Controlled elections

Strengthended police power

However, he also focused heavily on modernization and economic growth.

Modernization of France

Massive rebuilding of Paris under Baron Haussmann

Expansion of railways

Growth of banks and industry

Promotion of trade and capitalism

France became more industrial and economically modern during this time.

Later Rule: Liberal Empire (1860-1870)

In the 1860s, Napoleon III began allowing:

More press freedom

Greater power to the legislature

More political debate

This shift was meant to gain public support, but it also made political opposition stronger.

Foreign Policy and Fall

Napoleon III tried to expand France's influence abroad. Some actions included:

Supporting Italian unification

Invtervention in Mexico (which failed)

The biggest mistake came in 1870 when France went to war with Prussia in the Franco-Prussian War. France was quickly defeated, and Napoleon III was captured at the Battle of Sedan.

In 1870, the empire collapsed and France declared the Third Republic.
